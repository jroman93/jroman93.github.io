A/B Test: Email Subscribers

The business goal is to increase email subscribers. A discount incentive has been approved for this test. The variable in this test is discount amount shown in dollars vs. percentages.

Here I am testing the likelihood of a visitor subscribing to the news letter based on discount amount format, percentage vs. dollars. In the control, I have displayed the discount amount in percentage. In the variation, I have displayed the discount amount in dollars. My hypothesis is that changing the discount amount to dollars will increase the chance of a visitor to subscribe to the news letter. I would include all visitors in the United States in this experiment and I would set this test to run for 2 weeks, based on the visits per day. After analyzing the data I found that changing the discount amount from percent to dollars increased new letter subscription based on the significant lift in response submissions on variation B and total increased email subscribers for this time frame.

A/B Test: Button Placement

The business goal is to increase traffic to the "new arrivals" section of the website. In this test I created content to go in the newsletter to achieve increased visits to the "new arrivals" section. The variable in this test is button position.

Here I am testing the likelihood of an email subscriber clicking "shop new arrivals" based on button placement. This content would appear in the weekly newsletter. In the control, I have displayed the button at the top of the screen. In the variation, I have button at the bottom of the screen. My hypothesis is that changing the button position to the top of the screen will increase clicks, acheiving more visits to the "new arrivals" section. I would set this test to run for 4 weeks, due to the subscription based of the newsletter. After analyzing the data I found that changing the button position from the bottom of the screen to the top of the screen increased click rate and therefore increased visitor traffic to the "new arrivals" section of the website. An interesting point to note is typically visitors open the email newsletter via a mobile device.

Drip Campaign: Cold Lead Nurture

This is the first email in the drip campaign. The business goal is to nurture leads that have gone cold. These leads visited the website 3 months ago and since have not had a new visit. The subject line includes the prospects name followed by "did you forget to turn off your straightener?"

This is the first email in a cold lead nurture drip campaign. This email features content that is not product or promotional focused to re-engage the prospect. The subject line is personalized to increase chances of opens and the question evokes an emotion that everyone that has ever used a hair straightener has felt. The content aims to offer advice to the prospect and encourages a blog visit at the bottom of the page. If a prospect engages with the email by clicking on a blog post, the prospect will be removed from the next drip email and added to a new campaign.
